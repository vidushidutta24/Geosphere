Venus

Earth's Twin – The Hottest Planet

Venus is the second planet from the Sun and is nearly the same size as Earth, earning it the nickname "Earth's twin." However, beneath its thick clouds lies a hellish world. Its atmosphere is made mostly of carbon dioxide with clouds of sulphuric acid, creating a runaway greenhouse effect that makes Venus the hottest planet in the Solar System — even hotter than Mercury. Strangely, Venus rotates backwards compared to most planets, so the Sun rises in the west there.

Fascinating Facts

  • 🔥 Venus is the hottest planet, with surface temperatures reaching 465°C — hot enough to melt lead.
  • 🌀 Its day is longer than its year — Venus takes 243 Earth days to rotate once.
  • ↩️ Venus rotates backwards; the Sun rises in the west and sets in the east.
  • ☁️ Its thick clouds of sulphuric acid reflect sunlight, making it the brightest planet in our sky.
  • 🌍 Venus and Earth are almost the same size, but their atmospheres are completely different.
