Space Missions

Humanity has sent dozens of spacecraft across the Solar System. These brave missions have transformed our understanding of the cosmos and expanded our knowledge of worlds beyond Earth.

Historic Missions

Landmark missions that changed our view of the Solar System

🌕

Apollo 11 – Moon Landing

NASA  |  1969  |  Destination: Moon

The first crewed mission to land on the Moon. On July 20, 1969, Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin became the first humans to walk on the lunar surface, while Michael Collins orbited above. "One small step for man, one giant leap for mankind."

🚀

Voyager 1 & 2

NASA  |  1977  |  Destination: Outer Solar System & beyond

Launched in 1977, the twin Voyager spacecraft performed flybys of Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, and Neptune. Voyager 1 is now the most distant human-made object, having crossed into interstellar space in 2012.

🛸

Cassini-Huygens

NASA / ESA  |  1997–2017  |  Destination: Saturn

Cassini orbited Saturn for 13 years, revealing the planet's magnificent ring system in extraordinary detail. The Huygens probe descended through Titan's atmosphere and landed on its surface, sending back data about this moon's alien landscape.

🔭

Hubble Space Telescope

NASA / ESA  |  1990–present  |  Destination: Earth orbit

Hubble has been one of the most productive science instruments ever built. From its position above the atmosphere, it has provided breathtaking images of distant galaxies, nebulae, and planets, revolutionising our understanding of the universe.

🛰️

International Space Station (ISS)

NASA / ESA / Roscosmos / JAXA / CSA  |  1998–present  |  Destination: Low Earth Orbit

The ISS is the largest human-made structure in space, assembled in low Earth orbit over more than a decade. It serves as a microgravity research laboratory and has hosted hundreds of astronauts from 19 countries since its first crew arrived in November 2000, conducting thousands of scientific experiments in biology, physics, and astronomy.

🌠

Pioneer 10 & 11

NASA  |  1972–2003  |  Destination: Outer Solar System

Pioneer 10 was the first spacecraft to travel through the asteroid belt and fly by Jupiter, while Pioneer 11 followed and became the first to visit Saturn. Both carry a golden plaque depicting a man, a woman, and their origin — a greeting to any intelligent life that might one day find them drifting through the galaxy.

Recent & Ongoing Missions

The latest frontier of space exploration

🤖

Perseverance Mars Rover

NASA  |  2020–present  |  Destination: Mars

Perseverance landed in Jezero Crater on Mars in February 2021. It is searching for signs of ancient microbial life, collecting rock samples, and testing oxygen production from the Martian atmosphere. It also carries Ingenuity — the first powered aircraft to fly on another planet.

🌌

James Webb Space Telescope (JWST)

NASA / ESA / CSA  |  2021–present  |  Destination: L2 orbit

The successor to Hubble, JWST observes the universe in infrared light. Since becoming operational in 2022, it has delivered unprecedented images of galaxies, nebulae, and exoplanet atmospheres, looking further back in time than any previous telescope.

🪐

Juno – Exploring Jupiter

NASA  |  2011–present  |  Destination: Jupiter

Juno has been orbiting Jupiter since 2016, studying its atmosphere, magnetic field, and aurora. It has revealed new details about Jupiter's interior structure and provided stunning close-up images of the gas giant's swirling cloud bands.

🛩️

New Horizons – Pluto Flyby

NASA  |  2006–present  |  Destination: Pluto & Kuiper Belt

New Horizons performed the first flyby of Pluto in July 2015, revealing a geologically active world with mountains of ice, vast plains of nitrogen, and blue skies. It has since continued into the Kuiper Belt, studying other distant objects.
