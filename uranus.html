Uranus

The Tilted Giant – Sideways Ice World

Uranus is the seventh planet from the Sun and is classified as an ice giant. What makes Uranus truly unusual is that it rotates on its side — with an axial tilt of about 98 degrees. This means its poles experience 42 years of sunlight followed by 42 years of darkness. Uranus has a pale cyan-blue colour due to methane in its atmosphere, which absorbs red light. It has 13 known rings and 27 known moons.

Fascinating Facts

  • 🌀 Uranus rotates on its side — it essentially rolls around the Sun rather than spinning upright.
  • ❄️ Uranus is the coldest planet in the Solar System, with temperatures reaching -224°C.
  • 💎 Scientists believe it may rain diamonds deep inside Uranus due to extreme pressure.
  • 🌊 14 Earths could fit inside Uranus, making it the third largest planet by diameter.
  • 🔭 Only one spacecraft, Voyager 2, has ever visited Uranus — in 1986.
