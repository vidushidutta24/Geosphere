Neptune

The Distant Blue – Windiest World

Neptune is the eighth and farthest known planet in the Solar System. It is an ice giant, similar in composition to Uranus, with a vivid deep blue colour caused by methane in its atmosphere. Neptune is known for having the strongest winds of any planet — up to 2,100 km/h — and features a large storm called the Great Dark Spot. It has 16 known moons, the largest being Triton, which orbits backwards and is thought to be a captured Kuiper Belt object.

Fascinating Facts

  • 💨 Neptune has the fastest winds in the Solar System, reaching speeds of 2,100 km/h.
  • 🌑 Triton orbits Neptune backwards (retrograde), suggesting it was captured from the Kuiper Belt.
  • 🔭 Neptune was predicted mathematically before it was ever observed through a telescope.
  • 📅 A year on Neptune lasts 165 Earth years — only one Neptune year has passed since its discovery.
  • 🚀 Only Voyager 2 has visited Neptune, flying by in 1989.
