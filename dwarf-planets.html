Dwarf Planets

The Solar System has five officially recognised dwarf planets — small worlds that orbit the Sun but have not cleared their orbital neighbourhood of other debris, unlike full planets.

Pluto

Once considered the ninth planet, Pluto was reclassified as a dwarf planet in 2006 by the International Astronomical Union. It sits in the Kuiper Belt beyond Neptune. Its most famous feature is a large heart-shaped region of nitrogen ice called Tombaugh Regio. NASA's New Horizons spacecraft flew past Pluto in 2015, giving us our first detailed images.


Distance from Sun: 5.9 billion km  |  Diameter: 2,377 km  |  Moons: 5

Eris

Eris is the most massive and second largest known dwarf planet in the Solar System. Its discovery in 2005 directly led to the reclassification of Pluto. Eris is located in the scattered disc — a region far beyond the Kuiper Belt. It has one known moon, Dysnomia. Eris takes 559 Earth years to complete one orbit around the Sun.


Distance from Sun: Up to 14.6 billion km  |  Diameter: ~2,326 km  |  Moons: 1

Ceres

Ceres is the largest object in the asteroid belt between Mars and Jupiter, and the only dwarf planet in the inner Solar System. It is also the smallest of the five recognised dwarf planets. NASA's Dawn spacecraft orbited Ceres and discovered mysterious bright spots on its surface — later identified as highly reflective salt deposits.


Distance from Sun: 414 million km  |  Diameter: 945 km  |  Moons: 0

Makemake

Makemake is a dwarf planet located in the outer Solar System in the Kuiper Belt. It is the second brightest Kuiper Belt object after Pluto. Named after the creator god of the Rapa Nui people of Easter Island. In 2016, astronomers discovered Makemake has a small, dark moon, nicknamed MK2.


Distance from Sun: 6.85 billion km  |  Diameter: ~1,430 km  |  Moons: 1

Haumea

Haumea is a Kuiper Belt dwarf planet with one of the fastest rotation rates of any large body in the Solar System — completing a rotation in just under 4 hours. This rapid spin has stretched it into a distinctly elongated, egg-like shape. In 2017, astronomers discovered that Haumea has a ring system, making it the only known Kuiper Belt object with rings.


Distance from Sun: 6.48 billion km  |  Diameter: ~1,560 km  |  Moons: 2
